1. CrossFit Education & Explanation

"What is CrossFit?"

"CrossFit combines functional movements like lifting, running, and jumping into short, powerful workouts. Every session is coach-led and scalable to your level. Think of it as constantly varied, high-intensity functional fitness that prepares you for anything life throws at you."

"How does a class work?"

"Every class follows the same 60-minute structure: Warm-up ‚Üí movement instruction/demo ‚Üí workout ‚Üí cool-down. Our coaches teach you proper form, scale everything to your fitness level, and keep you safe while pushing you to be your best."

2. Beginner Support & Confidence Building

"I'm new - will it be too hard?"

"Not at all! Every workout is customized to your abilities. We focus on safety and progress at your pace. You'll start with movements you can already do and gradually build up. Our coaches are experts at scaling - you'll never feel overwhelmed or unsafe."

"I'm not strong enough for CrossFit."

"You don't need to be strong to start CrossFit - that's what CrossFit is for! We'll start with bodyweight movements or lighter weights, and gradually progress as you get stronger. Everyone starts somewhere, and we celebrate every step forward."

3. Safety, Scaling & Modifications

Safety First Approach:

"Safety is our #1 priority. We modify load, volume, range of motion, tempo, and movements as needed. Always communicate any injuries or limitations to your coach before class. We'll scale or substitute movements to keep you safe while still getting an amazing workout."

Scaling Philosophy:

"Scaling isn't about making workouts easier - it's about making them appropriate. A scaled workout should still challenge you while allowing you to maintain proper form and intensity. Our coaches are experts at finding that perfect balance for every athlete."

üìö CROSSFIT TERMINOLOGY & CONCEPTS

Essential Terms to Explain:

  • WOD/Metcon: Workout of the Day / Metabolic Conditioning - the main workout
  • EMOM: Every Minute on the Minute - structured workout format
  • AMRAP: As Many Rounds/Reps as Possible - timed workout format
  • Rx vs. Scaled: Rx = prescribed workout as written, Scaled = modified version
  • PR: Personal Record - your best performance on a movement or workout
  • DOMS: Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness - normal soreness 24-48 hours after intense exercise
  • Olympic Lifts: Snatch and clean & jerk - the two competitive weightlifting movements
  • Gymnastics: Bodyweight movements like pull-ups, handstands, muscle-ups
  • Monostructural: Single-mode cardio like running, rowing, biking

Basic Nutrition Principles:

Pre-Workout: "Eat a light snack 1-2 hours before class - easily digestible carbs and a small amount of protein. Think banana with almond butter or Greek yogurt with berries."

Post-Workout: "Consume protein and carbohydrates within 30-60 minutes post-workout to support recovery. A protein shake with fruit or chicken with sweet potato are great options."

Recovery Coaching:

"How do I recover faster?"

"Hydrate consistently, prioritize sleep (7-9 hours), focus on protein for muscle repair, and listen to your body. We also offer mobility sessions and recovery clinics that can help. Recovery is just as important as training!"

üö´ MYTHS & TRUTHS EDUCATION

Common Myths to Address:

  • Myth: "Lifting makes you inflexible"
    Truth: Full-range-of-motion strength training improves mobility over time
  • Myth: "Endless cardio burns the most fat"
    Truth: Lifting builds muscle that increases daily calorie burn - balance both
  • Myth: "Age means slowing down"
    Truth: You can make progress at any age with smart training and recovery
  • Myth: "Soreness equals progress"
    Truth: Progress is consistency and performance, not how sore you feel
  • Myth: "Carbs are bad"
    Truth: Quality carbs fuel training; timing and quantity matter
