What is CrossFit?

CrossFit is constantly varied functional movements performed at relative intensity. It's measurable and scalable. At Trident Athletics, our CrossFit classes incorporate elements from various sports and exercises including weightlifting, gymnastics, running, rowing, and more. The goal is to improve overall fitness across ten domains: cardiovascular endurance, stamina, strength, flexibility, power, speed, coordination, agility, balance, and accuracy. All workouts are scalable to your fitness level.

Do I Need to Be in Shape Before Starting CrossFit?

No, you don't need to be in shape before starting CrossFit! CrossFit is designed to be scalable to all fitness levels. Every workout can be modified to match your current abilities. Our experienced coaches will help you scale movements appropriately, whether you're a complete beginner or an experienced athlete. The beauty of CrossFit is that it meets you where you are and helps you progress at your own pace.

Is CrossFit Safe for Beginners?

Yes, CrossFit is safe for beginners when taught by qualified coaches like ours at Trident Athletics. All movements are scalable, and proper form is emphasized over intensity. Our coaches are trained to teach proper technique and provide modifications for any movement. Safety is our top priority, and we'll never push you beyond your capabilities.

How Often Should I Do CrossFit?

For beginners, we recommend starting with 3-4 classes per week to allow your body to adapt and recover. More experienced athletes may train 5-6 times per week. Rest and recovery are just as important as training, so listen to your body and work with your coaches to find the right frequency for your goals and lifestyle.

What Should I Eat Before and After CrossFit?

Before CrossFit: Eat a light snack 1-2 hours before class, focusing on easily digestible carbohydrates and a small amount of protein. Examples include a banana with almond butter or Greek yogurt with berries. After CrossFit: Consume protein and carbohydrates within 30-60 minutes post-workout to support recovery. A protein shake with fruit or chicken with sweet potato are good options.

Can I Do CrossFit if I Have Injuries?

Many people with injuries can safely participate in CrossFit with proper modifications. We recommend consulting with your healthcare provider first, then discussing your limitations with our coaches. We can modify or substitute movements to work around your injury while still providing an effective workout.

What's the Difference Between CrossFit and Regular Gym Workouts?

CrossFit differs from traditional gym workouts in several ways: it combines multiple fitness domains in one workout, uses functional movements that mimic real-life activities, emphasizes high intensity with varied workouts, and builds a strong community atmosphere. Traditional gym workouts often focus on isolated muscle groups or single fitness components.

Will I Get Bulky from CrossFit?

CrossFit will make you stronger and more athletic, but getting "bulky" depends on your nutrition, genetics, and training intensity. Most people find that CrossFit helps them build lean muscle while losing fat, creating a toned, athletic physique rather than a bulky one.

How Long Are CrossFit Classes?

Most CrossFit classes are 60 minutes long, including warm-up, the workout of the day (WOD), and cool-down. This allows for proper preparation, the main workout, and recovery time.

What Equipment Do I Need to Start CrossFit?

You don't need any special equipment to start! Just bring comfortable workout clothes, supportive athletic shoes, a water bottle, and a towel. All the equipment you need is provided at the gym.

What Should I Expect in My First CrossFit Class?

Your first class will include a brief facility tour, introduction to our coaches and community, and a workout scaled to your fitness level. We'll focus on proper form and technique while introducing you to our supportive environment. Our coaches will take time to understand your goals and fitness background to ensure you have the best possible experience.

Is CrossFit Good for Weight Loss?

Yes, CrossFit can be very effective for weight loss when combined with proper nutrition. The high-intensity workouts help burn calories during and after exercise, while building muscle that increases your metabolism. Our coaches can also provide nutrition guidance to support your weight loss goals.

Can Older Adults Do CrossFit?

Absolutely! CrossFit is scalable for all ages. We have members of all ages, and our coaches are experienced in modifying workouts for older adults. The focus on functional movements can actually help improve mobility, balance, and strength as you age.

What if I'm Not Strong Enough for CrossFit?

You don't need to be strong to start CrossFit - that's what CrossFit is for! Our coaches will scale every movement to your current strength level. You might start with bodyweight movements or lighter weights, and gradually progress as you get stronger.

How Do I Know if CrossFit is Right for Me?

The best way to find out if CrossFit is right for you is to try it! We offer trial classes so you can experience our community and coaching style before committing. CrossFit is for everyone, but the community and coaching style at each gym can vary, so it's important to find the right fit.

What Should I Wear to CrossFit?

Wear comfortable, breathable workout clothes that allow for a full range of motion. Avoid baggy clothes that might get caught on equipment. Good athletic shoes with a flat sole are recommended. Leave jewelry at home for safety.

How Do I Track My Progress in CrossFit?

We track progress in many ways: improved times on workouts, increased weights lifted, better form, increased endurance, and how you feel overall. Our coaches will help you set realistic goals and track your progress over time.

What if I Can't Do a Movement?

That's completely normal! Every movement in CrossFit can be scaled or substituted. Our coaches will show you modifications or alternative movements that work the same muscle groups but are appropriate for your current fitness level.

How Much Does CrossFit Cost?

CrossFit memberships vary by location and membership type. At Trident Athletics, we offer various membership options to fit different budgets and goals. Contact us for current pricing and to discuss which membership option works best for you.

Do I Need to Be Competitive to Do CrossFit?

Not at all! While CrossFit has a competitive side, most people do CrossFit for general fitness and health. You can be as competitive or non-competitive as you want. The community aspect is often more important than competition for many members.

CrossFit Terminology

Understanding common CrossFit terms: WOD/Metcon (Workout of the Day/Metabolic Conditioning), EMOM (Every Minute on the Minute), AMRAP (As Many Rounds/Reps as Possible), Rx (prescribed workout as written), Scaled (modified version), PR (Personal Record), DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness), Olympic lifts (snatch and clean & jerk), gymnastics movements (pull-ups, handstands, etc.), and monostructural work (running, rowing, biking).

Safety and Scaling

We modify load, volume, range of motion, tempo, and movements as needed. Always communicate any injuries or limitations to your coach before class. Safety is our top priority, and we'll scale everything appropriately for your fitness level.

Common Myths and Truths

Myth: "Lifting makes you inflexible." Truth: Full-range-of-motion strength training improves mobility over time. Myth: "Endless cardio burns the most fat." Truth: Lifting builds muscle that increases daily calorie burn - balance both. Myth: "Age means slowing down." Truth: You can make progress at any age with smart training, nutrition, and recovery. Myth: "Soreness equals progress." Truth: Progress is consistency and performance, not how sore you feel.
